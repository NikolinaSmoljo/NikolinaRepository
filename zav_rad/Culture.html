Cetina Region

Part of the Dalmatian hinterland (about 1000 km2) between the two parallel mountain ranges Svilaja and Dinara (a massif comprising Ilica, Dinara Troglava and Kamešnica), with the Cetina River, which flows through the long valley made up of fields Cetina, Vrlika, Hrvatac and Sinj. Koljanac and Ribarica Fields are now under water Peruca Lake. Cetina rises from five karst "vrila" in the form of a dark lake, the bottom of a deep sinkhole from which the entire river flows, 380 m above the sea between Gnjat (1809 m) and Dinara (1831 m), two kilometers north of Vrlika.

Runs through narrow gorges, spreading through Sinj Field, before quickening its pace through the canyons that form a semi surround range of Moser to the mouth near Omis. It is believed that her Hippus ancient name comes from the upper course of the river - the Greek "hippos" = "horse", because quick navigable upper river like a horse. Archaeological remains (hill-fort and lake-dweller settlements and necropolis the stone cairns) testify to the upper and middle stream Cetina was the central and most important part Delmatae territory.

Cetina Region Museum

Founded in 1956, the center is a place of research and presentation of the rich cultural - historical heritage of the Cetina region. The museum includes the archaeological, numismatic, cultural - historical, ethnographic and natural history collection and the collection of weapons, photographs and photographic equipment, documents, postcards, posters and art collection of mostly artists from the Cetina region. Particularly important ancient monuments rich architectural decoration, altars dedicated to the Roman gods and tombstones from Ruduše that are from the 1st century, and valuable because of Illyrian names and motifs.
