Ham is the king of the specialties of the Dalmatian hinterland and one of the most original product of Croatian cuisine. This is a pig's leg dried on wind and smoke, which is preceded by a complex process of preparation which includes proper separation, washing and salting, calendering, coating a mixture of flour, pepper, paprika and ash quality wood. Smoking takes 70 days, and the total drying of 120 days. Dalamatian ham

Traditional production of Dalmatian smoked ham comes from a cottage industry in Dalmatia, from Starigrad to the Bay of Kotor and deep in the area of the Dalmatian hinterland. The primary treatment and the way of salting meat, depended of the customs and habits of the population. The final result was evident in the uneven quality, taste, smell, color and consistency of ham. Dalmatian smoked ham can become the brand and achieve success in the EU market, according to its shape, weight, excellent taste, smell, consistency, color and marbling. This ham can be produced only with strictly controlled breeding pigs appropriate breeds. Production of Dalmatian smoked ham is not possible to establish a bad import meat or pale, soft and exudative meat. Dalmatian smoked ham after 12 months has top quality, recognizable and protected under the name "Dalmatian prosciutto".
